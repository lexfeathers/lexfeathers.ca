My friend Nicole commissioned a portfolio site from me with a catch: it needed to have modular blocks for laying out pages, and be responsive and well-documented so she could easily add content and build layouts on the fly. 

![A page from Nicole Calzavara's website shown in light mode](/uploads/DesignByNicole_page1.png)

[Lume](https://lume.land) was my choice of static site generator, and [Vento](https://vento.js.org) was the templating language I used. For the modular "blocks" I used Lume components, which work similarly to JSX components. 

By referencing figma wireframes provided by Nicole, I developed each component along with thorough documentation, ensuring they would be flexible and responsive so she could use them for a variety of purposes.

![Nicole's about page on mobile, with a hamburger menu in the header](/uploads/DesignByNicole_mobile1.png)

The resulting site feels clean, flexible in its layout capabilities, and dynamic. This was my first time creating a modular design framework from the ground up, and I had a great time coming up with all kinds of ways to make adding content to the site modular, without impeding creativity.
